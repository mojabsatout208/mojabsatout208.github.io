How to Protect Yourself

01

Know the infection vectors

Malware arrives through unsafe downloads, suspicious email attachments, fake websites, or compromised USB drives.

Phishing linksFake appsUSB dropsDrive-by downloads
02

Use strong passwords

At least 12 characters — uppercase, lowercase, numbers, symbols. A unique password for every site. Use a password manager.

Password managerEnable 2FANever reuse
03

Don't ignore updates

Many attacks exploit known vulnerabilities — ones that updates would have already patched. Updates = protection.

Auto-update onUpdate appsUpdate OS
04

Antivirus & Firewall

A firewall monitors network traffic. Antivirus software scans for known malware. Together they provide real protection.

Windows DefenderFirewall activeScan regularly
05

When in doubt — don't click

If a message, link, or file feels off — just don't click it. Legitimate companies never ask for passwords via email.

Check the senderInspect the URLTrust your gut
// Glossary

Cybersecurity Terms

👆
Tap a card to reveal the definition6 key terms you need to know — tap any card to expand it.
🎣
Phishing
Dangerous deception
Fake emails or websites that look identical to real ones — goal: steal your login credentials. "We're your bank, please confirm your password."
🧱
Firewall
Your digital bouncer
Monitors and filters all data traffic between your device and the internet. Automatically blocks unwanted connections.
🔐
Encryption
Making data unreadable
Data is converted into unreadable code — only someone with the correct key can read it. WhatsApp uses this to protect your messages.
🔑
2FA
Two-factor security
Two-factor authentication: besides your password you also need a second code via SMS or app. Even if someone steals your password — without your phone, they're locked out.
🕵️
Social Engineering
Hacking humans
Attackers manipulate people instead of systems. "I'm from IT support — I need your password for a second." Humans are often the weakest link.
🛡
Zero-Day
Unknown vulnerability
A security flaw nobody knows about yet — not even the manufacturer. Extremely dangerous because no patch exists. Hackers sell zero-days for hundreds of thousands of euros.
